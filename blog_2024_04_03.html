HOW TO AVOID THE SKILL LOSS TRAP

Have you ever suddenly just ‚Äúlost‚Äù a skill that you had a week ago?

To me, this is usually a sign of someone who is working when they are training rather than learning.

In any sort of movement practice, doing a skill mindfully 2 times will get you the same results as if you did a skill 10 times "just to do it".

The body should never be considered in isolation from the mind.

In dance and in flexibility, I never have my students set goals that are visual or number oriented. I have them set goals that are based on body awareness and control. What are they more aware of today versus yesterday? What amount of control has changed?

This helps them understand what progression or element needs to be worked on when they've lost a skill they had before.

It makes their training 10x more efficient.

And most of all, it builds the foundation for them to do anything and not just the particular skill we are working on.
