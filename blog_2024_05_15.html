UNMASKING THE REAL YOU

A long time ago, I showed one of my competition pieces to a coach for feedback. I remember being so proud of that piece; the technique, choreography, and character were all there. Like a child eager for approval and validation from a mother, I showed her my video.

“Your piece is good, but I don’t really see you in it.”

My heart dropped.

What did she mean by she didn’t really see me? I chalked it up to the fact that I didn’t have a “style” and so I dedicated the next few years to developing one.

Today, that memory has surfaced repeatedly in my thoughts as I explore what it means to have sensual truth.

What do you do for you?

What do you do for others?

I’ve realized that I edit my dance for how it will be perceived.

Until recently, I didn’t know the difference between dancing for others and dancing for yourself-like REALLY know the difference.

Even when I dance with the intention for what “feels good”, I limit myself to what also “looks good”.

And the craziest thing? I didn’t even notice this until last week.

I know now, why she could not see me.

Even after 5 years of developing my own style, freestyling, and tapping into my emotions, I am at a loss when it comes to what is truly me.
